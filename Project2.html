VitaPrint
Vision
     We empower individuals to take control of their health by providing them with the technology to “print” their daily, personalized multivitamin. The majority of the American population is micronutrient-deficient and would benefit from supplementation. These deficiencies differ from person to person, which has directed the supplement industry to provide fragmented yet generic consumer products. On the contrary, we are confident that we can design both the software necessary to determine an individual’s particular deficiencies and the hardware that allows the customer to print a personalized multivitamin from the comfort of their own home.
Progress
     We have made progress on hardware design. Concurrently, we are researching critical concepts; primarily powder flow behavior. As of now we’ve modeled theoretical powder flow equations and begun to apply concepts to refine our design. We have also brainstormed a potential financial model with multiple targets for selling units: home devices, drug store kiosks, and corporate vending machines.

      We have explored the United States Department of Agriculture nutrient database, and utilized their API to gather data necessary to determine individuals’ deficiencies. Software has been outlined to utilize this data along with a potential user’s biometric stats to determine their necessary dosages of a vitamin. We have also researched potential ways to mitigate the amount of user input by considering partnerships with external companies such as as SmartPlate, Vitastiq, and/or 23andme.

      Our concrete goal is to have a working prototype with software and hardware cohesion. This entails an Arduino program processing input, and controlling motors that dispense measured amounts of powdered vitamins and minerals into a consumable pill.

      We are also in the process of applying to accelerators, on and off campus. We hope to secure mentorship, a workspace, and an initial budget to develop our working prototype.
